It gives any service organization – IT, HR, Facilities, Telecom, Government, etc. – one simple way to: - Turn **wishes, changes, incidents, requests, and risks** into predictable results - Standardize **processes, workflows, teams, and tooling** - Improve **quality, control, and customer experience** while managing cost This site brings together: - Executive-level explanation - Chapter-by-chapter summaries of the USM book - Visual models (processes, workflows, decision tree, “subway maps”) - [Engagements & services](#engagements) - [Contact](#contact) --- ## Chapter Summaries {#chapters} ### Chapter 1 – Why Services & Why USM? - Modern organizations create value mainly **through services**, not products - USM adopts a **Service-Dominant (S-D) logic**: value is co-created with the customer - Defines key concepts: service, value, customer experience, quality - Shows why we need **one simple, generic system** instead of many fragmented frameworks --- ### Chapter 2 – Principles & Architecture - USM is built on a small set of **business principles** (e.g. “structure by segmenting and managing relationships”) - Introduces the **enterprise service management architecture** (ESMA) - Defines **5 generic processes** and **8 workflows** as the core of the management system - Explains the **maturity/value perspectives**: control, quality, cost, compliance, customer value --- ### Chapter 3 – Customer, Services & Agreements - Focus on **customer-driven service delivery** and co-creation of value - Defines services, service units, and the **service catalog** - Explains agreements: - SA (Service Agreement) – with the customer - OLA (Operational Level Agreement) – internal - UC (Underpinning Contract) – external provider - Introduces **SERVQUAL** and **UX/CX** as lenses for perception of quality --- ### Chapter 4 – People & Organization Basics - Distinguishes between: - **Process model** (how work should flow) - **Organization model** (how people and teams are arranged) - Introduces **functions, roles, profiles, TAR** (Tasks, Authorities, Responsibilities) - Shows why a **matrix organization** works well for services - RACI is positioned as a way to link **profiles ↔ process steps** --- ### Chapter 5 – Processes & Process Model - The 5 USM processes: - **CTM – Agree** (contract/relationship management) - **CHM – Change** - **INC – Recover** - **OPS – Operate** - **RIM – Improve / Risk** - Shows the **color-coded process model** and how triggers (wish, RFC, incident, service request, risk) flow through it - RIM “wraps around” the other four processes as a continuous improvement loop ![USM Process Stack](/assets/images/usm/usm-process-model.png) --- ### Chapter 6 – Workflows & Activities - Processes are turned into **8 standardized workflows** that cover all reactive & proactive work - Each workflow is a **fixed sequence of generic steps** (accept, classify, specify, plan, execute, evaluate) - Workflows are reusable patterns: - Same structure across all domains - Different content per organization - Enables **tooling and templates** to be set up once and reused widely --- ### Chapter 7 – The Service Organization - Connects USM to classical organization topics: - **Mission, vision, core values, strategy, objectives** - **Governance vs management** - **Planning & control** at strategic, tactical, operational levels - Explains **domain separation** (specify vs realize), **separation of duties**, and drama triangles - Describes **sourcing, outsourcing, insourcing, multisourcing** and their risks - Details **teams, profiles, RACI, service desk**, and dangers of combining conflicting roles --- ### Chapter 8 – Technology Resources - Describes the **resource spectrum**: - Service delivery coordination (SDC) / service desk tools - MIR (Managed Infrastructure Register) - Knowledge, PPM, reporting, monitoring, intranet, etc. - Compares **ERP vs Best-of-Breed** strategies - Defines requirements for a **USM-capable SDC tool**: - Workflow templates for each USM workflow - Single call database - Integrated MIR - Self-service portal - Reporting & interfaces --- ### Chapter 9 – Deploying USM - USM deployment = **learning to apply the method** - Phasing: 1. Intake & scope 2. Planning & setup 3. First improvement sprint 4. Ongoing sprints (“Learn” phase) 5. Transfer to standing organization - Organizational change management is central (attitude, behavior, culture) - Shows how USM works **with** Agile, DevOps, Lean, Design Thinking, and other techniques - Explains USM’s role in **shared service centers (SSC/iSSC)** and **compliance with standards** (ISO, COBIT, etc.) --- ## USM Process & Workflow Model {#model} USM has **5 processes** (vertical blocks) and **8 workflows** (horizontal patterns that cross those processes). ![USM Processes & Workflows Overview](/assets/images/usm/usm-workflows.png) **Processes (colors):** - **Blue – CTM (Agree):** services, catalog, and agreements with customers & suppliers - **Yellow – CHM (Change):** structured change of the managed infrastructure - **Red – INC (Recover):** restore service after a failure or incident - **Green – OPS (Operate):** planned, routine delivery of agreed services - **Orange – RIM (Improve):** risks, improvements, and continual learning These processes are combined into **8 workflows**, which we’ll visualize below. --- ## Workflow Decision Tree {#decision-tree} The decision tree helps you select the correct workflow quickly by asking a few simple questions: 1. Is the trigger **reactive or proactive**? 2. Does it relate to the **agreement**, a **failure**, or an **action**? 3. Does the action require a **change to the managed infrastructure**? ![USM Workflow Decision Tree](/assets/images/usm/usm-workflow-decision-tree.png) ![USM Workflow Decision Tree Detail](/assets/images/usm/usm-workflow-decision-tree-detail.png) The colored blocks at the top of each branch show which **workflow** you’ll use (1–8) and which processes are involved (CTM, CHM, INC, OPS, RIM). --- ## Workflow Subway Maps {#subway-maps} Each workflow is drawn like a **subway line**: same sequence of steps, different “stations” (activities) per organization. ### Workflow 1 – Agreement Update / Issue Triggered when: > There is an issue in the agreement or the agreement needs to be updated. ![Workflow 1 subway map](/assets/images/usm/usm-workflow-1-subway-map.png) --- ### Workflow 2 – Action Requiring Change Triggered when: > The caller needs an action **and** that action requires a change to the managed infrastructure. ![Workflow 2 subway map](/assets/images/usm/usm-workflow-2-subway-map.png) --- ### Workflow 3 – Failure Requiring Change Triggered when: > There is a failure **and** recovery requires a change. ![Workflow 3 subway map](/assets/images/usm/usm-workflow-3-subway-map.png) --- ### Workflow 4 – Failure Recovery (No Change) Triggered when: > There is a failure, but recovery does **not** require a change to the managed infrastructure. ![Workflow 4 subway map](/assets/images/usm/usm-workflow-4-subway-map.png) --- ### Workflow 5 – Action (No Change) Triggered when: > The caller requests an action that does **not** require a change. ![Workflow 5 subway map](/assets/images/usm/usm-workflow-5-subway-map.png) --- ### Workflow 6 – Wish Assessment Triggered when (proactive): > There is a **wish** for something new or different. ![Workflow 6 subway map](/assets/images/usm/usm-workflow-6-subway-map.png) --- ### Workflow 7 – Proactive Change Triggered when (proactive): > A planned **change** is needed (e.g., improvement, preventive work). ![Workflow 7 subway map](/assets/images/usm/usm-workflow-7-subway-map.png) --- ### Workflow 8 – Proactive Service Request / Measure Triggered when (proactive): > A **service request / measure** arises from risk management or improvement work. ![Workflow 8 subway map](/assets/images/usm/usm-workflow-8-subway-map.png) --- ## Engagements & Services {#engagements} USM is most powerful when it’s translated into **simple, practical routines** that fit your organization. Executive Briefing & Vision Alignment - 2–3 hour interactive session (onsite or remote) - Explains the 5 processes, 8 workflows, and decision tree in **business language** - Links USM to: - Governance & risk - Customer satisfaction - Tooling and silos - Outcome: shared understanding and **clear “why”** for USM --- ### 2. USM Quick Scan & Roadmap - Short assessment of: - Current services and agreements - Processes, teams, and tools - Pain points and risks - Uses USM maturity/value model (control, quality, cost, compliance) - Delivers a **3–6 month roadmap** with prioritized, realistic steps --- ### 3.
