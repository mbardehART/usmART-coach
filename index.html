# USM Coaching & Advisory Practical guidance for Service Management Excellence Default language: English (Farsi & Arabic available) EN فارسی العربية --- ## About USM Unified Service Management (USM) is a method that enables service organizations to structure, standardize, and continuously improve the way services are delivered — using one universal management system that applies across all domains. USM focuses on people, processes, and technology — and provides repeatable ways to convert customer needs into predictable, high-quality results. --- ## USM Workflow Decision Tree This decision model helps you select the correct workflow based on whether the trigger is reactive or proactive, and whether it relates to an agreement, a failure, or an action. ![USM Workflow Decision Tree](/assets/images/usm/usm-workflow-decision-tree.png) ![USM Workflow Decision Tree Detail](/assets/images/usm/usm-workflow-decision-tree-detail.png) --- ## USM Process & Workflow Model All USM workflows follow the same structured logic — enabling clarity, repeatability, and measurable outcomes across every service domain. ![USM Processes and Workflows Overview](/assets/images/usm/usm-workflows.png) --- ## Engagements & Services {#engagements} USM is most powerful when it’s translated into **simple, practical routines** that fit your organization. I typically work with clients in a few flexible ways: ### 1. Executive Briefing & Vision Alignment **Goal:** Give leaders a clear, shared picture of USM and how it supports their strategy. - 2–3 hour interactive session (onsite or remote) - Explains the 5 processes, 8 workflows, and decision tree in business language - Connects USM to your current challenges (governance, tooling, silos, customer experience) - Suitable for: boards, CIO/CTO, heads of IT / Operations / Facilities --- ### 2. USM Quick Scan & Roadmap **Goal:** Understand where you are today and identify the most valuable first steps. - Short assessment of your current processes, teams, and tools - Uses the USM maturity and value perspectives (control, quality, costs, compliance) - Delivers a **prioritized improvement roadmap** (3–6 months) with concrete actions - Ideal when you are: - Preparing for outsourcing / insourcing - Considering a new service desk / ESM tool - Merging teams into a shared service center --- ### 3. Guided USM Deployment & Coaching **Goal:** Implement USM step-by-step, with your own people in the driver’s seat. - Follows the USM deployment phasing (intake → set-up → improvement sprints → handover) - Focus on: - Defining services and agreements (CTM) - Designing workflows and RACI (CHM, INC, OPS, RIM) - Configuring your SDC / ESM tool to support USM workflows - Coaching style: “learn by doing” – your team applies USM on real cases, not theory - Can start small (one team or domain) and grow to enterprise level --- ### 4. Tooling & Workflow Design Support **Goal:** Make your service desk / ESM tool truly reflect USM – instead of the other way around. - Support for designing: - Call types and workflows - Templates for wishes, changes, incidents, service requests, risks - MIR structure (services, MICs, relationships) - Neutral guidance: I don’t sell tools – I help you get the most from the one you choose. --- ### 5. Training, Workshops & Coaching Circles **Goal:** Build internal capability so USM becomes “how we work”, not “a project”. - Role-based sessions (service managers, process/workflow managers, tool owners, service desk) - Scenario-based exercises using your own examples - Optional ongoing **USM coaching circle** for key practitioners and champions If you’d like to explore which engagement fits you best, just reach out via the contact section below. --- ## The 8 USM Workflows Each workflow is color-coded and numbered.
